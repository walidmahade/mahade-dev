Nine times out of ten when a client asks "Webflow vs Shopify" the answer is "both" — a Webflow marketing site in front of a Shopify backend. That setup runs the best-of-both for most direct-to-consumer brands. The tenth time, there is a real decision to make. Here is how to read your situation.

## At a glance

| | **Webflow** | **Shopify** |
|---|---|---|
| Primary strength | Marketing site | Commerce backend |
| Checkout | Native, basic | Native, best-in-class |
| Payment providers | Stripe, PayPal, a few more | Dozens, native Shop Pay |
| Inventory mgmt | Simple | Enterprise-grade |
| Product variants | Limited | Flexible, deep |
| Multi-currency | Limited | Native Markets |
| App ecosystem | Small | Enormous |
| Design freedom | Very high | Medium (theme-bounded) |
| CMS | Yes, strong | Yes (Metaobjects) |
| Best for | Brand-led sites under 50 SKUs | Real stores at any scale |

## The real question: what is the *primary* job of the site?

If the main job is **to sell**, pick Shopify. Every inch of Shopify is optimized for moving from product page to confirmed order. Cart abandonment recovery, multi-channel selling, international tax, shipping zones, and the Shop Pay one-click checkout — all native.

If the main job is **to market and convert leads** (and selling is occasional or low-SKU), Webflow makes more sense. You get full design control, a proper CMS for editorial content, and the option to bolt on Webflow Ecommerce or embed Shopify Buy Buttons.

## Design freedom

Webflow is a visual development platform; design freedom is the whole point. Shopify themes have come a long way, and Shopify's Online Store 2.0 with Liquid + app blocks now allows real customization — but you are customizing *within a theme*, not building up from HTML/CSS.

For brand-led sites where the design language is distinctive, Webflow is the more comfortable canvas.

## The hybrid model (and why it works)

The pattern I recommend most often:

1. **Webflow handles the marketing site.** Home, about, editorial content, case studies, campaigns — everything designed with full freedom and managed in Webflow CMS.
2. **Shopify handles the store.** Product pages, cart, checkout, inventory, fulfillment.
3. **Connect them** with a subdomain (shop.yourbrand.com) pointing at Shopify, or embed the Buy Button SDK in Webflow, or use the Shopify Hydrogen approach for fully custom product pages.

This plays to each platform's strengths. The designer gets the control they want. The commerce team gets the backend they need. The customer gets a seamless branded experience.

## SEO

Both platforms emit clean HTML, support per-page meta, handle sitemaps, and let you customize robots. Shopify's product schema is automatic and excellent. Webflow's editorial schema is better suited to long-form content.

For a marketing-heavy site, Webflow's SEO surface wins. For product-heavy SEO (product schema, review aggregation, in-stock indicators), Shopify's defaults are hard to beat.

## Pricing

Not directly comparable:

- **Webflow** charges per site for hosting + CMS + Ecommerce plan.
- **Shopify** charges per store for the subscription + per-transaction fees on non-Shopify-payment gateways.

For a low-SKU brand, Webflow Ecommerce can be the same order of magnitude as Shopify Lite. For a real DTC brand, Shopify's per-transaction structure pays for itself in checkout conversion alone.

## Who should pick which

**Pick Webflow (standalone) if:**
- You sell fewer than 50 products
- The site is primarily a brand or marketing site
- Design freedom outranks commerce features
- You want one CMS for both editorial and products

**Pick Shopify (standalone) if:**
- You sell more than 100 products
- International checkout matters
- You need the app ecosystem (subscriptions, POS, shipping)
- The store *is* the site

**Pick both if:**
- You are a DTC brand that also publishes editorial content
- Your designer wants full control over marketing pages
- You are scaling past the early-stage store

## My take

I build Webflow sites for a living, and I still recommend Shopify the moment a client sells more than 50 SKUs or ships internationally. Trying to bend Webflow into a real storefront when Shopify exists is a false economy.

The hybrid model — Webflow front, Shopify back — is the right pattern for most brands over $500k in annual revenue. The setup is not free, but it pays back within the first holiday season.

## FAQ

**Can Webflow and Shopify share a domain?**
Yes. Host the marketing site on yoursite.com (Webflow) and the store on shop.yoursite.com (Shopify), or use Shopify headless with a Webflow front end and custom product pages.

**Does the hybrid model hurt SEO?**
No, if you plan it. Subdomain vs subdirectory is a live debate, but both rank fine. Just make sure internal linking is sensible and canonical tags are set.

**What about Webflow Ecommerce for a real store?**
It is capable for low-SKU brands. For serious ecommerce, Shopify's checkout is measurably better at conversion. That measurement will pay for every dollar of the migration.
